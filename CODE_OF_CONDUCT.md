# Code of conduct

## Our standard

Project discussions should be direct, respectful, technically grounded, and
welcoming to people with different backgrounds and levels of experience.
Review the work, not the person. Ask for evidence, explain trade-offs, and give
others a reasonable opportunity to correct mistakes.

Constructive participation includes:

- keeping issues and pull requests focused on reproducible behavior;
- stating assumptions and safety implications clearly;
- accepting corrections and review feedback in good faith;
- protecting credentials, private data, and unpublished security details;
- respecting licenses, attribution, and the authorship of others.

Unacceptable behavior includes harassment, threats, personal attacks,
discriminatory language, sexualized attention, deliberate intimidation,
doxing, sustained disruption, impersonation, or publishing another person's
private information without permission.

## Scope

This code applies in repository issues, pull requests, reviews, discussions,
release channels, and other spaces where someone is representing the project.

## Reporting and enforcement

For ordinary disagreements, pause and return to the technical facts. Report
serious or repeated conduct privately through
<https://github.com/ADIKANT/datalens-dev-mcp/security/advisories/new> with
"Code of conduct" in the title. Do not disclose sensitive personal details in
a public issue.

The maintainer may edit or remove comments, close or lock threads, reject
contributions, issue a warning, or temporarily or permanently restrict
participation. Decisions will consider severity, recurrence, impact, and the
safety of affected participants. Reports will be handled as privately as the
platform allows, subject to legal obligations.
